Nasi Kandar

Nasi Kandar is a popular northern Malaysian dish, which originates from Penang. It was popularized by Tamil Muslim traders from India. It is a meal of steamed rice which can be plain or mildly flavored, and served with a variety of curries and side dishes. Traditionally, nasi kandar is always served with its side dishes on a single plate. Nowadays, small melamine bowls are used for the side dishes. Nevertheless, the curry sauce mix is always poured directly onto the rice.


History

Hameediyah, is recognized as Penang's oldest nasi kandar restaurant, originally started under a tree at a field in Lebuh Campbell, Penang, in 1907. After 90 years, nasi kandar returned to Chennai, when Pelita Samudera Pertama opened a branch there. In 2022, the first Nasi Kandar restaurant opened in Japan, the first-ever nasi kandar speciality store serving authentic Malaysian-Indian cuisine in the country.
